MG-42 universal machine gun

Mg-42 General purpose Machine gun, short for German Maschinengewehr 42, meaning "Machine gun 1942" (" Maschinen "is" Machine "; "Gewehr" is "rifle"). The most prominent feature is the speed of fire, known as "Hitler's chainsaw." The MG-42 general-purpose machine gun was developed and put into service by Nazi Germany in 1942, basically based on the weapon using 7.92×57mm Mauser rifle as the basis of development, and was prepared to replace the MG34 general-purpose machine gun. However, the two machine guns were used together until the end of the war.
